Feats

A feat is a representation of a powerful skill or ability that a character can utilize. Feats go beyond the normal abilities received from class features. At certain levels your class gives you the Ability Score Improvement feature. Using the optional rule, you can forgo taking the feature to take a feat of your choice instead. You can take each feat only once, unless otherwise stated in the feat's description. You must meet any prerequisite specified in a feat to take that feat and if you lose a feat's prerequisite you can't use the feat until you regain the prerequisite. New feats are presented here in alphabetical order.

Bardic Adept

Prerequisite: Proficiency with a musical instrument

Your skill in song, speech and magic grants you new abilities. You gain the following benefits:

  • You learn one cantrip of your choice from the bard spell list
  • You gain one use of the Bardic Inspiration feature from the bard class. As a bonus action, you can target a creature other than yourself within 60 feet of you who can hear you. That creature gains one Bardic Inspiration die, which is a d6. Once within the next 10 minutes, the creature can roll the die and add the number rolled to one ability check, attack roll, or saving throw it makes. The creature can wait until after it rolls the d20 before deciding to use the Bardic Inspiration die, but must decide before the DM says whether the roll succeeds or fails. Once the Bardic Inspiration die is rolled, it is lost. A creature can have only one Bardic Inspiration die at a time. Once this feature is used, you can't use it again until you finish a short or long rest.
  • You can use a musical instrument you are proficient with as a spellcasting focus for any spell you cast that uses Charisma as its spellcasting ability.

Dark Acolyte

  • Prerequisite: Wisdom or Charisma 13 or higher

    • Your divine properties take on a sinister form. You gain the following benefits:

    • You learn one 1st-level necromancy spell of your choice. You can cast this spell without expending a spell slot. Once you cast this spell in this way, you can’t do so again until you finish a long rest. You can also cast the spell using any spell slots you have. The spellcasting ability for this spell is the ability that met the prerequisite for this feat (your choice if both met the prerequisite).
    • When you use an ability or cast a spell that causes a creature to regain hit points that has no effect or can't be used on undead, it now can be used on undead.
    • You can use the skull of a Medium or Small creature as a spellcasting focus for any spell you cast that uses Wisdom or Charisma as its spellcasting ability.

    Druidic Armor Master

    Prerequisite: At least 1 level in the druid class

    You learn an ancient ritual allowing you to turn metal to ironwood. You gain the following benefits:

    • You gain proficiency with heavy armor.
    • You can perform a 1-hour ritual to convert a set of metal armor or a metal shield into ironwood. This conversion does not change the statistics of the armor or shield.

    Heavens Touched

    Your exposure to the radiant energy of Mount Celestia has changed you, granting you the following benefits:

    • Increase your Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma score by 1, to a maximum of 20.
    • You learn the prayer of healing spell and one 1st-level spell of your choice. The 1st-level spell must be from the abjuration or divination school of magic. You can cast each of these spells without expending a spell slot. Once you cast either of these spells in this way, you can’t cast that spell in this way again until you finish a long rest. You can also cast these spells using spell slots you have of the appropriate level. The spells’ spellcasting ability is the ability increased by this feat.

    Hells Touched

    Your exposure to the horrific energy of the Nine Hells has changed you, granting you the following benefits:

    • Increase your Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma score by 1, to a maximum of 20.
    • You learn the crown of madness spell and one 1st-level spell of your choice. The 1st-level spell must be from the evocation or necromancy school of magic. You can cast each of these spells without expending a spell slot. Once you cast either of these spells in this way, you can’t cast that spell in this way again until you finish a long rest. You can also cast these spells using spell slots you have of the appropriate level. The spells’ spellcasting ability is the ability increased by this feat.

    Leap Attack

    Prerequisite: Great Weapon Master

    You've learned how to land a powerful overhead strike, taking advantage of a weapon's weight. You gain the following benefit:

    • On your turn, if you jump at least 10 feet horizontally before you make a melee attack with a heavy weapon that you are proficient with, you can choose to take a -5 penalty to the attack roll. If the attack hits, you add +15 to the attack’s damage. You can't gain the benefits of this feat and Great Weapon Master on the same attack.

    Paladin Initiate

    Prerequisite: Charisma 13 or higher

    You have started the path to becoming a divine warrior:

    • You learn one cantrip of your choice from the cleric spell list, and you learn one 1st-level spell of your choice from the paladin spell list. Charisma is your spellcasting ability for these spells.
    • You can cast this feat's 1st-level spell without a spell slot, and you must finish a long rest before you can cast it in this way again. You can also cast the spell using any spell slots you have.
    • You can use a holy symbol as a spellcasting focus for any spell you cast that uses Charisma as its spellcasting ability.

    Perfect Cavalry

    Prerequisite: Centaur

    You have mastered the form of being a mount and a warrior at the same time. You gain the following benefits:

    • Increase your Strength score by 1, to a maximum of 20.
    • You count as one size larger for the purpose of being treated as a mount and if a creature mounts you, you always continue to act independently. Additionally, for a creature to mount or dismount you it only costs 5 feet of movement, rather than half its speed.
    • If a creature fails its saving throw to stay mounted on top of you, you can make a DC 10 Strength saving throw to stop them from falling off.
    • When a creature mounted on you is forced to make a Dexterity saving throw, the creature gains a bonus to the saving throw equal to your Strength modifier.

    Ranger Initiate

    Prerequisite: Wisdom 13 or higher

    You begin to harness the power of nature magic:

    • You learn one cantrip of your choice from the druid spell list, and you learn one 1st-level spell of your choice from the ranger spell list. Wisdom is your spellcasting ability for these spells.
    • You can cast this feat's 1st-level spell without a spell slot, and you must finish a long rest before you can cast it in this way again. You can also cast the spell using any spell slots you have.
    • You can use a druidic focus as a spellcasting focus for any spell you cast that uses Wisdom as its spellcasting ability.

    Shield Combatant

    Prerequisite: Proficiency with shields and a martial weapon

    You learn to use a shield for both defense and offense. You gain the following benefits:

    • Increase your Strength score by 1, to a maximum of 20.
    • You can treat a shield you are wielding as a martial melee weapon with the versatile property and you gain proficiency with using shields as a weapon. On a hit, a shield will deal 1d8 bludgeoning damage, or 1d10 bludgeoning damage if used with two hands.
    • Once on each of your turns, you can choose to forgo the AC bonus granted by a shield you are wielding until the start of your next turn. When you do so, you can make an attack with the shield as a bonus action.

    Small Might

    Prerequisite: Small race and Strength 13 or higher

    You have trained so your size is no longer a detriment to your combat ability, gaining the following benefits:

    • You no longer suffer the penalty of wielding a heavy weapon due to your size category being Small.
    • You gain proficiency with one heavy weapon of your choice.
